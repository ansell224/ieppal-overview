Teachers have always had the data, so the problem is not collection, it's digesting it.

Before we wrote a single line of code for [iep-pal.com](http://iep-pal.com/), we spoke to 50+ educators across Singapore, Vietnam, Hong Kong, Cambodia, Denmark, and Holland. 

The same pattern kept surfacing: teachers weren't in a data void, they were drowning in data without context.

Progress notes scattered across three platforms and benchmark scores living in a spreadsheet. 

As I like to say, data must be readable at 7pm on a Tuesday.

This is what AI is actually changing in education right now.

A recent scoping review on AI and educational data analytics in K–12 IEP implementation found direct improvements in progress monitoring and intervention fidelity. Nearly 60% of special education teachers reported using AI for IEP development in the last school year, an 18-point jump in a single year.

AI and learning analytics are making it possible for a teacher to surface a student's progress trend across a semester in seconds. 

Don't believe us? We're building it right now :D
