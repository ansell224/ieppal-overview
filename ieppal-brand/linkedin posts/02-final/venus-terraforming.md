I was watched SpaceX's Starship Flight 12 this week, and I kept noticing an assumption buried underneath all of it.

Mars (and now the moon) is the plan. It's been the plan so long it barely reads as a science fiction anymore.

So one morning a week or two ago, I looked further into the stars and asked: What would it take to live on Venus?

If you didn't know, Venus has a 460°C surface. Atmospheric pressure 93x Earth's and the most extreme greenhouse effect in the solar system.

But strip that away, and Venus is almost exactly Earth's twin. 90% of our surface gravity. 95% of our radius. Inside the habitable zone.

It made me realize that the bottleneck isn't technical. It's generational.

The engineers who laid the foundations of medieval cathedrals never saw the spires. The planners of Rome's aqueducts built deliberately for people they'd never meet. They did it anyway.

The topic is interesting, but the underlying questions are even better.

What's the longest time horizon you've ever built toward? What does it feel like to work on something you know you won't finish?
