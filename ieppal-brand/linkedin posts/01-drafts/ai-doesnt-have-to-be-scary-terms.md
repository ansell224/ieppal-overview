Every time someone said "the model hallucinated," I used to picture a robot seeing things.

It took me longer than I'd like to admit to just... look it up.

This is part of a series I'm calling *AI Doesn't Have To Be Scary*. Each post, one concept — explained like you'd explain it to a friend.

Here are the 6 terms worth actually knowing first:

**1/ LLM (Large Language Model)**
The actual name for tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. A model trained on billions of words to predict what text should come next.

**2/ Hallucination**
When an AI sounds certain and is completely wrong. It's not lying — it's pattern-matching toward a plausible answer even when the facts don't exist.

**3/ Prompt**
What you type into an AI. The more specific your input, the better the output. You're already doing this. You can get much better at it.

**4/ Context Window**
The AI's working desk. Everything in your current conversation sits on it. Once the desk is full, older messages fall off — and the AI genuinely forgets them.

**5/ Token**
How AI measures text. About 4 characters = 1 token. This is why AI tools have usage limits — they're counting tokens, not words.

**6/ AI Agent**
An AI that takes actions, not just answers questions. It can browse the web, run code, draft emails, and plan steps on its own.

These six cover about 80% of what you'll encounter in the wild.
