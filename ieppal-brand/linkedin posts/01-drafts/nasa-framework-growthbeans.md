# NASA Framework — Growthbeans

**Topic:** NASA facilitation framework from Growthbeans; subtle IEP Pal connection

My default when someone brings me a problem is to jump straight to solutions.

I think I'm being helpful. But more often, I'm just rushing past them.

Last September, I took a facilitation training with [Growthbeans] that introduced me to a model I keep coming back to. Four letters: NASA.

**Notice** — the words someone uses, their tone, their body language. You can't notice any of it if you're not genuinely present.

**Acknowledge** — not the "at least you have X" kind. Real acknowledgement sounds like: "It must have been so hard to hear that so suddenly." It makes someone feel like their experience is real and valid, not a problem to be patched.

**Share** — verbalize what you noticed. A bit of encouragement fits here too, if it's genuine.

**Ask** — questions that help someone arrive at their own solutions. They're more likely to follow through because *they* suggested it.

What I keep returning to is that N and A in particular require something I often underestimate: genuine, undivided presence. The kind that's hard to give when your attention is already stretched thin.

I've been applying this in the men's growth circles with Growthbeans, and it's made me more intentional about what kind of attention I'm actually offering when someone opens up. Still practicing. But it's made the conversations noticeably different.

It's also shaped how I think about building IEP Pal — not as something that replaces the relational parts of an educator's work, but as something that might quietly create a little more space for them.

What frameworks have changed how you show up for the people around you?
