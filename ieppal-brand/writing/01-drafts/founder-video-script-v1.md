# IEP Pal — Founder Video Script
**Format:** Ansel + Ian, talking-head / conversation  
**Target length:** 1–2 minutes  
**Audience:** Investors  
**Tone:** Warm and personal — leaves the viewer feeling they've just met two people who genuinely understand the problem and have the conviction to solve it.

---

## Pacing guide
At a natural conversational pace (~130 words/min), 1–2 minutes = roughly 130–260 spoken words.  
This outline runs lean so you have room to breathe and be yourselves on camera.

---

## SECTION 1 — Why we started IEP Pal
*~35–40 seconds. Ansel opens. Ian follows with the firsthand angle.*

**Ansel — suggested sentences / talking points:**
- I had teachers who made time for me. Not because they had to — because the system wasn't eating all of their time. That's not something every kid gets. And I've never stopped thinking about that gap.
- The students who get the most out of school are often the ones whose teachers could *actually* show up for them — not just compliance-wise, but relationally.
- We started IEP Pal because paperwork was standing between educators and the kids who needed them most.

**Ian — suggested sentences / talking points:**
- I was in the classroom. I saw what IEP season looked like from the inside — the hours, the Sunday nights, the cognitive load of trying to do right by every student while meeting every deadline.
- It wasn't a lack of care. It was a lack of time.
- That's the problem we're solving.

---

## SECTION 2 — Why we're the right people to build it
*~30–35 seconds. This should feel like two complementary perspectives, not a credentials list.*

**Ansel — suggested sentences / talking points:**
- I come at this from the student side. I know what it means to be on the receiving end of an educator who had *space* to notice you.
- My job is to make sure we never lose sight of who we're really building for — the relationship, not just the workflow.

**Ian — suggested sentences / talking points:**
-I come at it from inside the system. I know the forms, the jargon, the actual process of writing a compliant IEP under pres sure.
- Between us — one person who benefited from educators having time, one person who lived the cost of not having it — we're not guessing at the problem.

**Suggested closer (either of you, or together):**
- We're yin and yang on this. That's not accidental. That's the whole point.

---

## SECTION 3 — Long-term vision
*~25–30 seconds. End on the student and the educator — not on the product.*

**Suggested sentences / talking points:**
- The long-term vision isn't about software. It's about what happens when educators get time back.
- When a teacher isn't spending her Sunday writing goals, she's spending it on the student she's worried about. That's the outcome we're working toward.
- We want IEP Pal to be the tool that quietly handles the paperwork — so the teacher can be the one who actually shows up.
- If we do this right, students will feel it before they ever know the product exists.
- Data is powerful. But it's always been the relationship that changes a kid's life. We want to build the thing that protects both.

---

## Optional closing line (if you want a single memorable line to end on)
> *"The students who need the most support deserve educators who have the space to give it. IEP Pal is how we get there."*

---

## Editing notes
- Don't over-rehearse. The warmth has to feel real — investors will read it immediately if it's scripted-sounding.
- Let Ian own the classroom texture. Let Ansel own the "why this matters beyond the tool" framing.
- Avoid saying "AI-powered" or "solution." Say what the tool *does*: drafts, tracks, organizes, saves time.
- One moment of genuine vulnerability will do more than three strong data points. Pick one true thing and say it plainly.
