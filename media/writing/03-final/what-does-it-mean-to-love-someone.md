# What does it mean to love someone?

Every night since as long as I can remember, my routine has looked something like this: I brush my teeth, ready my bed, and head to say goodnight to my parents. Our goodnights have ranged from a hug, a light conversation, and on occasion, tears or a panic attack. The one constant of every single goodnight was the words uttered, "I love you." Often, we take love as something for granted, or we take it as something ethereal, but in both cases, it is equally real and present.

On a good night many months ago, as I felt the warmth of my father's hug, I wondered what it really meant to love.

I thought for a few moments, and then for a few weeks, but I could not articulate a definitive answer. My simple question perplexed me: What exactly does it mean to love someone?

A few weeks ago, I sat down at a cafe in Paris, across from my beautiful girlfriend. I paused to soak in the moment, being abroad, enjoying the company of someone I love. As she smiled at me, I wondered, What made me love her? What made her love me? Parents (If you're lucky) give you unconditional love, but she didn't have to love me. What made up our love? Perhaps a sort of two-way street, but one without a centerline — where love could change any direction, at any moment, for any reason.

A few days prior, we had an argument that made both of us quite emotional; yet, sitting in that café, I couldn't imagine my life without her. I certainly couldn't imagine loving her any less. So, as I stared at the middle ground between her eyes and my bacon cheeseburger, my answer started to unfold.

Loving someone means… Loving them even when you're upset with them.

We all say sh*t that unintentionally upsets those we love, and you likely have been on the receiving end of these comments and actions; however, learning to forgive, even when you're upset, is what it means to love someone.

Loving someone means… Sharing your deepest fears and listening to theirs.

If love is a plant that must grow, vulnerability is the water and sunshine that help it along the way. To truly love someone, you must put a frighteningly large amount of trust in them. For them to love you, you must bear the responsibility of helping protect who they are.

Loving someone means… Being willing to hear what you're scared to admit.

We all have things we're afraid to admit, but loving someone means you listen to them when they bring it up. Sometimes it's in the heat of an argument, and sometimes they want to learn more about you at your core. Sacrificing your short-term comfort for the ability to love and be loved, regardless of what you fear, is what love is.

Loving someone means… thinking before you speak, and sometimes, not thinking before you speak.

Recognize what emotions you feel. If you are angry or frustrated, choose your words and actions carefully. If you are anxious or ruminating, sometimes saying everything is your best option. Honesty is a pillar that helps love grow.

Loving someone means… sometimes putting down what feels like the opportunity of a lifetime for them.

I exaggerate slightly, though it may feel like that sometimes. Loving someone means giving up something and being okay with that. It could be a job, a career, or an activity you really want to do.

Loving someone means… Truly understanding their needs.

I'm not talking about what coffee they like, though that is important. How do they want to be spoken to? What tone of voice do they not appreciate? How many hugs would they like each day? Do they want to be greeted when they come home?

Loving someone means… Giving really good hugs.

I recognise that not everyone is a hugger, so replace it with what you'd like. It could be a warm meal or a joint activity. Find what gets you as close together as a hug.

Loving someone means… Realizing they don't need a problem solved, they just need someone to listen.

Sometimes those you love just need to vent. They don't need their life problems to be solved; they want someone to understand their experience. If you can't lend a hand, lend an open ear.

If you've gotten to the end and thought of a few more that should be on this list, that was my intention. Loving someone, and being loved by someone, is a spectrum that expands the width of the universe. What does loving someone mean to you?
